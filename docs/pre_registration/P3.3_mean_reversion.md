## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable)

On the frozen large-cap **panel** at 5-minute bars, **fading statistically stretched intraday moves back toward a rolling mean** — shorting when the close is `> +entry_z` standard deviations above an **intraday-reset** rolling mean, buying when `< −entry_z` below, exiting when it reverts to within `exit_z` of the mean — produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** clearing the **two-part panel verdict**: (1) the equal-weight panel-portfolio clears the seven-point kill-gate (CPCV median path-Sharpe > 1.0, net of the full Indian round-trip cost, realized-frequency annualized) AND (2) breadth holds (median per-symbol CPCV path-Sharpe > 1.0 and ≥ 60% of panel symbols individually positive).

**Falsified if** the panel verdict is KILL/INSUFFICIENT, or the cost-viability pre-check (§6) finds the median gross per-trade move below the modeled round-trip cost.

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern) — and honest low prior

Short-horizon **overreaction** in liquid names tends to revert: a burst that pushes price several σ from its recent intraday mean is partly liquidity demand (an impatient order, a stop cascade), and liquidity provision earns the snap back toward fair value. The z-score band is the **stretch measure**; the wide-entry / narrow-exit hysteresis captures the reversion *toward* the mean, not merely the excursion beyond entry (which would be cost-dead). Prior evidence (`RESEARCH_FINDINGS.md` §4.2) rates this "the most persistently documented short-horizon effect, but the edge is small and easily eaten by intraday costs" (catastrophic in trends/news; fat left tail; the fade fights momentum), so **KILL is a live and expected outcome.**

**Honest low prior, stated up front (per operator guidance).** P3.3 is **another mean-reversion fade, like P3.1 V1 — but a DIFFERENT anchor.** V1 faded deviation from **intraday VWAP** (volume-weighted) and **KILLed**; P3.3 fades deviation from a **rolling SMA normalized by rolling σ** (the canonical Bollinger / z-score anchor). Distinct falsifiable hypothesis, but **prior interest is low** — mean-reversion already died on the VWAP anchor. Stated **ahead of the result**:

- A **KILL reinforces** "no intraday mean-reversion edge on large-caps" (the anchor was not the reason V1 died).
- A **PASS where V1 KILLed** is an **anchor-specific finding to investigate** (the statistical-band anchor works where VWAP did not) — **not a contradiction** (same *direction*, different anchor; a fade passing does not contradict another fade failing). Like any pass it triggers the **verdict-grade path** (backfill the 5 current members, re-run on the full universe) before it is a real candidate.
- P3.3 **correlates with V1** (both fades), so the two **cluster** in the trial ledger — the cluster-adjusted effective-N will not double-count them as independent bets.

## 3. Spec (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

Implemented as `MeanReversionSpec` in [`src/lab/research/strategies/mean_reversion.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/mean_reversion.py), registered `mean_reversion` — deterministic and point-in-time.

- **Signal:** the **intraday-reset rolling z-score** `z = (close − intraday_mean) / intraday_std` over the last `lookback` bars **of the current IST day** (`indicators.intraday_zscore`), a full `lookback`-bar window required (NaN in each day's warmup), population σ (Bollinger convention). A signal at bar `t`'s close fills at bar `t+1`'s open (Inviolable Rule 2).
- **Entry (fade):** from flat, `z > +entry_z` ⇒ **SHORT**; `z < −entry_z` ⇒ **LONG**.
- **Exit (hysteresis):** hold until `|z| ≤ exit_z` — a SHORT exits when `z ≤ exit_z`, a LONG when `z ≥ −exit_z`. Wide-entry / narrow-exit mirrors P3.1 V1's structure.
- **Holding / square-off:** intraday only; each day opens flat; positions squared off at the **15:20** MIS cutoff. No stop-loss in this study (an ATR stop would be a separate ledger-charged variant).
- **Position size:** fixed unit (`strength = 1.0`).

**Design ruling — INTRADAY-RESET, not a trailing window (operator, 2026-07-09).** The rolling mean/σ reset at the IST day boundary (a new `intraday_zscore` indicator, tested like `intraday_donchian`), NOT the trailing `bollinger`. An intraday fade must measure stretch against **TODAY's** mean; a trailing window would read an **overnight gap** as a huge z-score and fade **against the gap**, quietly turning P3.3 into a partial **gap-fade** — a different strategy (near the inverse of P3.10 Gap-and-Go). That is a leakage of the wrong reference level, not a simplification. Same correctness principle already applied to breakout (`intraday_donchian`) and VWAP (resets by construction).

## 4. Pre-committed parameters (FROZEN before the run, set BLIND)

| Parameter | Value | ± one-step (sensitivity) | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| `entry_z` | **2.0** | ±0.5 | z-score stretch beyond which the fade is entered (2σ textbook Bollinger) |
| `exit_z` | **0.5** | ±0.25 | z-score within which the fade is exited (near the mean) |
| `lookback` (N) | **20** bars | — (fixed) | intraday rolling window for the mean + σ (≈100 min at 5-min) |
| position size | **1.0** (fixed) | — | no sizing |

**Frozen and chosen BLIND** (no data peeking). The ± one-step neighbours drive criterion-6a parameter sensitivity and the PBO configuration matrix — **5 configs**: base + `entry_z±` + `exit_z±` (each satisfies `0 < exit_z < entry_z`); `lookback` fixed. The registry entry (`STRATEGIES["mean_reversion"]`) holds these exact values (pinned by `tests/unit/test_registry.py`). **Every variant is charged to the trial ledger**, K aggregate-portfolio streams per study (panel is scope, not N×K trials).

**Why 5-minute is natural for this fade specifically (mechanical, given the intraday-reset ruling).** Because the z-score window is **intraday-reset**, a full 20-bar window must form since the day's open before any fade can fire. At 5-min a 20-bar window ≈ 100 min (formed by ~10:55, ~4h25m of session left to fade and revert). At 15-min it spans ~5h of the ~6h regular session (formed ~14:15, ~1h left) — **mechanically non-viable for this lookback**, not merely less preferred. Finer than 5-min (1/3-min) inflates z-score noise → false fades + turnover/cost. **So the reserved coarser-frequency follow-up is NOT a "same strategy, slower bar" variant: at 15-min a 20-bar window is unusable, so any coarser-bar look requires a DIFFERENT `N` — a re-parameterized strategy, charged to the ledger as its own variant and labeled as such, run only if the 5-min primary shows life.** (Same shape as the P3.2 breakout frequency argument — both use an intraday-reset window.)

**Variant coverage (method-choice rule).**
- **No owed directional dichotomy:** P3.3 *is* the fade direction; "continuation off the band" is the breakout bet (P3.2, already KILLed). Nothing owed.
- **z-score ≡ Bollinger %B:** a Bollinger band at `k·σ` is a z-score of `k`; they are one mechanism, not two variants — no split is manufactured.
- **Anchor = rolling SMA** (VWAP is the separate P3.1). An **EMA anchor** or alternative normalization is a deferred ledger-charged method variant, run only if the SMA primary shows life.
