## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable)

On liquid NSE large-cap equities at 5-minute bars, entering in the **direction of a decisive break of the prior N-bar range** — a close above the prior-N-bar high (LONG) or below the prior-N-bar low (SHORT) — **filtered by a relative-volume surge** (`rel_vol > volume_mult`), and **holding the continuation** until an opposite filtered break flips it or intraday square-off closes it, produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** that clears the seven-point kill-gate (CPCV median path-Sharpe > 1.0, net of the full Indian round-trip cost).

**Falsified if** the kill-gate returns KILL, or the cost-viability pre-check (§6) finds the median gross per-trade move smaller than the modeled round-trip cost. Prior evidence (`RESEARCH_FINDINGS.md` §4.2) rates breakouts "mixed — unfiltered breakouts historically decay; volume/volatility filters help but rarely enough to clear costs intraday" (high false-break rate, whipsaw in chop, slippage worst exactly at the break), so **KILL is a live and expected outcome.**

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern)

A trading range is a **temporary equilibrium** — buyers and sellers balanced within `[prior low, prior high]`. A **decisive break on expanding participation** signals that the equilibrium has resolved: resting liquidity at the level is absorbed, stops trip, and momentum/breakout traders are drawn in, so price **continues** in the break direction. The **volume filter is the crux** (per §4.2, "volume confirmation is the crux"): a break on high relative volume is more likely a genuine resolution than a low-volume drift through the level, which tends to be a **false break** that reverts — the filter is what the whole hypothesis rests on.

**Two things stated up front, honestly (per operator guidance):**

- **Relationship to P3.1 (VWAP-fade), a reason to test — not to expect a pass.** Breakout is the **opposite directional bet**: VWAP-fade bet on *reversion* from stretched levels and **KILLed** (2026-07-09) precisely because, in this data, price tends to **continue** from stretched levels rather than revert (win rate 0.48). So the VWAP KILL is **mild prior evidence of interest** in a continuation bet — a reason to run breakout — but it is **not** a reason to expect it to pass. The parameters below are set **blind** (no data peeking); the gate rules. The prior-evidence view still rates breakouts as rarely clearing costs intraday.
- **Not double-dipping, and the honest interpretation if both were to pass.** VWAP-fade and breakout are **near-opposite directional bets on the same underlying phenomenon** (price behaviour at stretched / extended levels). Testing both is **not** double-dipping — they are **distinct falsifiable hypotheses** (fade: reverts; breakout: continues), each judged once by the same unchanged gate. But stated **ahead of the result**: if **both** were to clear the gate, that is a **contradiction to investigate, not two independent wins** — the same phenomenon cannot robustly both revert and continue net-of-cost. (VWAP-fade already KILLed, so this is moot for now; the discipline is pre-committed regardless.)

## 3. Spec (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

Implemented as `BreakoutSpec` in [`src/lab/research/strategies/breakout.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/breakout.py) — deterministic and point-in-time (the prior-N-bar high/low is the rolling Donchian channel **as of the previous bar**, `donchian(...)[i-1]`, so it excludes the current bar; relative volume uses only prior volumes; a signal at bar `t`'s close fills at `t+1`'s open).

- **Signal:** prior-N-bar high `= donchian_upper[i-1]`, prior-N-bar low `= donchian_lower[i-1]`; relative volume `= volume[i] / mean(prior volume_period volumes)` (`indicators.relative_volume`, excludes current).
- **Entry (filtered break):** if `rel_vol > volume_mult` **and** `close > prior_high` ⇒ **LONG**; if `rel_vol > volume_mult` **and** `close < prior_low` ⇒ **SHORT**. A break without the volume surge is **ignored** (the false-break guard).
- **Exit / holding:** ride the continuation intraday — the position is held (forward-filled) until an **opposite filtered break** flips it or the **15:20 MIS square-off** closes it. Each day opens flat; no overnight carry. No mid-range flat exit and **no stop-loss** in this study (an ATR stop is a separate, later idea — P4.6 — and would be a new ledger-charged variant here).
- **Position size:** fixed unit (`strength = 1.0`). No sizing model.

## 6. Cost-viability pre-check (before the full battery)

Same methodology as P3.1: before the full battery, confirm the edge can clear costs at all.

- **Measure:** the base-config backtest's **median `|Trade.gross_return|`** (pre-cost per-trade move) on the primary symbol.
- **Compare to:** `cost_model.round_trip_cost_fraction(100_000)` ≈ **0.18%**.
- **Decision:** median gross per-trade move **< round-trip cost** ⇒ **cost-dead**; record that as the verdict, log the trial, **skip the full battery**. Otherwise proceed.
- **A-priori note (NOT a result):** because the spec **holds the continuation to square-off** rather than scalping the break, a *genuine* breakout can capture a multi-bar move (potentially > 0.3%), so it is **not obviously cost-dead** on move size; the danger is directional (false breaks reversing), which the full gate — not the pre-check — tests. The measured median governs.
