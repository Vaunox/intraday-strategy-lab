## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable)

On the frozen large-cap **panel** at 5-minute bars, entering in the **direction of a decisive break of the prior N-bar INTRADAY range** — a close above the prior-N-bar high (LONG) or below the prior-N-bar low (SHORT), the range computed over **current-day bars only** (`intraday_donchian`, resets at the IST day boundary) — **filtered by a relative-volume surge** (`rel_vol > volume_mult`), and **holding the continuation** until an opposite filtered break flips it or the 15:20 square-off closes it, produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** that clears the **two-part panel verdict**: (1) the equal-weight panel-portfolio clears the seven-point kill-gate (CPCV median path-Sharpe > 1.0, net of the full Indian round-trip cost, realized-frequency annualized) AND (2) breadth holds (median per-symbol CPCV path-Sharpe > 1.0 and ≥ 60% of panel symbols individually positive).

**Falsified if** the panel verdict is KILL/INSUFFICIENT, or the cost-viability pre-check (§6) finds the median gross per-trade move smaller than the modeled round-trip cost. Prior evidence (`RESEARCH_FINDINGS.md` §4.2) rates breakouts "mixed — unfiltered breakouts historically decay; volume/volatility filters help but rarely enough to clear costs intraday" (high false-break rate, whipsaw in chop, slippage worst at the break), so **KILL is a live and expected outcome.**

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern)

A trading range is a **temporary equilibrium** — buyers and sellers balanced within `[prior low, prior high]`. A **decisive break on expanding participation** signals the equilibrium has resolved: resting liquidity at the level is absorbed, stops trip, and momentum/breakout traders are drawn in, so price **continues** in the break direction. The **volume filter is the crux** (per §4.2): a break on high relative volume is more likely a genuine resolution than a low-volume drift through the level — the classic **false break** that reverts.

**Intraday reset matters to the mechanism:** the level being broken is TODAY's range, formed since the open, not a level carried across the overnight gap. Using an intraday-reset range keeps this a *prior-N-bar-range* breakout, distinct from Opening-Range Breakout (P3.11, the day's running high/low) — the two must stay different strategies (an expanding day-range here would collapse them into one correlated bet and corrupt the trial-count accounting).

**Two things stated up front, honestly (per operator guidance):**

- **Relationship to P3.1 (VWAP-fade), a reason to test — not to expect a pass.** Breakout is the **opposite directional bet**: VWAP-fade bet on *reversion* from stretched levels and **KILLed** (2026-07-09 pilot; superseded) precisely because, in this data, price tends to **continue** from stretched levels rather than revert. So that KILL is **mild prior evidence of interest** in a continuation bet — a reason to run breakout — but **not** a reason to expect it to pass. Parameters are set **blind**; the gate rules.
- **Not double-dipping, and the honest interpretation if both were to pass.** VWAP-fade and breakout are **near-opposite directional bets on the same phenomenon** (price at stretched levels). Testing both is **not** double-dipping — distinct falsifiable hypotheses — but stated **ahead of the result**: if **both** clear the gate, that is a **contradiction to investigate, not two independent wins**.

## 3. Spec (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

Implemented as `BreakoutSpec` in [`src/lab/research/strategies/breakout.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/breakout.py) — deterministic and point-in-time (`intraday_donchian`: the prior-N-bar high/low over current-day bars only, excluding the current bar, NaN at each day's first bar; relative volume uses only prior volumes; a signal at bar `t`'s close fills at `t+1`'s open).

- **Signal:** prior-N-bar high/low = `intraday_donchian(ohlcv, breakout_lookback)[i]` (current-day, as-of bar `i`, excludes `i`); relative volume = `volume[i] / mean(prior volume_period volumes)` (`indicators.relative_volume`, excludes current).
- **Entry (filtered break):** `rel_vol > volume_mult` **and** `close > prior_high` ⇒ **LONG**; `rel_vol > volume_mult` **and** `close < prior_low` ⇒ **SHORT**. A break without the volume surge is **ignored** (false-break guard). The day's first bar has no intraday range (NaN) → no cross-gap break.
- **Exit / holding:** ride the continuation intraday — held (forward-filled) until an **opposite filtered break** flips it or the **15:20 MIS square-off** closes it. Each day opens flat; no overnight carry. No mid-range flat exit and **no stop-loss** in this study (an ATR stop is a separate later idea; here it would be a new ledger-charged variant).
- **Position size:** fixed unit (`strength = 1.0`). No sizing model.

## 6. Cost-viability pre-check (before the full battery)

Same methodology as P3.1: confirm the edge can clear costs at all before the full battery.

- **Measure:** the base-config backtest's **median `|Trade.gross_return|`** (pre-cost per-trade move) on the primary panel name (RELIANCE, for continuity), regular-session filtered.
- **Compare to:** `cost_model.round_trip_cost_fraction(100_000)` ≈ **0.18%**.
- **Decision:** median gross per-trade move **< round-trip cost** ⇒ **cost-dead**; record that as the finding, log the trial, skip the full battery. Otherwise proceed to the full panel verdict.
- **A-priori note (NOT a result):** because the spec **holds the continuation to square-off** rather than scalping the break, a *genuine* breakout can capture a multi-bar move (> 0.3%), so it is **not obviously cost-dead** on move size; the danger is directional (false breaks reversing), which the full gate — not the pre-check — tests.
