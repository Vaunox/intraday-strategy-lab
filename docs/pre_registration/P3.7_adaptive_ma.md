## 0. Two variants owed (directional dichotomy) — the coverage decision, explicit

Adaptive MAs are a genuine **directional dichotomy** (`MASTER_BLUEPRINT.md:327`, "cross or slope"), so **BOTH are owed** — two specs, two runs, each pre-registered; **neither defaulted, neither deferred**:

- **V1 — Cross (crossing signal):** position = the cross of a **fast and a slow KAMA** (`sign(KAMA_fast − KAMA_slow)`).
- **V2 — Slope (trend-state signal):** position = a **single KAMA's own direction** (`sign(KAMA − KAMA_prev)`).

**Correction on record (2026-07-09) — the coverage decision, proven not asserted.** An initial V1 definition (a *price-vs-single-KAMA* cross) was found **mathematically identical to V2's slope**: a KAMA always sits between its prior value and the close, so `sign(close − KAMA) ≡ sign(KAMA − KAMA_prev)` **every bar** — the two would have been the *same* strategy. The degeneracy was caught **pre-run** by the divergence precondition test (0 divergent bars), and V1 was redefined as a **fast/slow cross of two KAMAs**, which breaks the identity (the fast KAMA can lead above the slow while the slow KAMA still falls — a reversal-lead a single MA cannot have). **The divergence is PROVEN empirically:** the fast/slow cross and the single-KAMA slope disagree on **~34.5% of real RELIANCE bars** (`test_adaptive_ma_spec.py` pins non-zero divergence). This is the "prove it, don't assert it" discipline earning its keep — confident MA-divergence reasoning was flatly wrong; only the empirical test was decisive.

**On record: if BOTH pass, that is a contradiction to investigate, not two wins** — now genuinely meaningful, since the two signals demonstrably disagree a third of the time.

**Distinct from P3.14 (Moving-Average Crossover):** P3.14 crosses *fixed* SMA/EMA; V1 here crosses two *efficiency-ratio adaptive* KAMAs — adaptive vs fixed smoothing, genuinely different, so P3.14 must not later be run as a near-duplicate of V1.

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern) — and honest low prior

A Kaufman Adaptive MA (KAMA) **speeds up in trends and slows in chop** (via the efficiency ratio), so it tracks a genuine trend closely and flattens in noise — the "noise-adaptive" property the study is about.
- **V1 (cross):** a **fast** adaptive MA crossing **above/below a slow** one marks a directional regime shift (the faster MA leads the slower) → trade the cross direction.
- **V2 (slope):** the adaptive MA *pointing* up/down is itself the trend-state → trade the direction it points.

**Honest low prior.** Trend/**continuation** family. **Every continuation bet so far has KILLed** (P3.1 V2 VWAP-cross, P3.2 breakout, P3.6 Donchian), and `RESEARCH_FINDINGS.md` §4.2 rates adaptive MAs "marginal improvement over fixed MAs; not a reliable standalone edge," so **KILL is a live and expected outcome for both.** One honest note, explicitly **not a thumb on the scale:** the **slope variant is the closest yet to the long-horizon-continuation direction** the P3.6 "continuation gradient" pointed at (a pure trend-state follower on a noise-adaptive MA = the cleanest trend-following bet in the slate). That is informative *context*, **not** a reason to expect a pass — the gradient's read was "direction of least-bad-ness informative, magnitude firmly dead."

## 3. Specs (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

Both in [`src/lab/research/strategies/adaptive_ma.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/adaptive_ma.py); both use the **trailing** KAMA (`indicators.kama` = `talib.KAMA`, the same period per variant).

- **V1 — `AdaptiveMaCrossSpec`** (registered `adaptive_ma_cross`): per bar, `KAMA_fast > KAMA_slow` ⇒ **LONG**, `KAMA_fast < KAMA_slow` ⇒ **SHORT**, flat while either KAMA is NaN (warmup) or they are equal. The cross of a **fast and a slow** KAMA (two adaptive MAs — see §0 for why a single-KAMA price cross is degenerate). Each day opens flat; 15:20 square-off.
- **V2 — `AdaptiveMaSlopeSpec`** (registered `adaptive_ma_slope`): per bar, `KAMA[i] > KAMA[i−1]` (rising) ⇒ **LONG**, `KAMA[i] < KAMA[i−1]` (falling) ⇒ **SHORT**, flat while KAMA is NaN or exactly flat. The position follows the KAMA's own direction. Each day opens flat; 15:20 square-off.
- **Position size:** fixed unit (`strength = 1.0`).

**Design ruling — TRAILING KAMA, not intraday-reset (operator, 2026-07-09).** Both variants use the standard trailing `talib.KAMA` (no daily reset). **The P3.3 intraday-reset analogy does NOT transfer:** (a) a trailing KAMA is **causal** — reflecting yesterday's level at today's open is legitimate *past* information, not lookahead, so it is not a leak; (b) intraday-reset would **amputate the cross-day adaptivity that is the defining property of an adaptive MA** (KAMA's identity is adapting its speed to the multi-day regime) and discard the first ~50 min of every session to warmup — testing a *crippled cousin*, not the indicator. So this fork is "standard, keeps the adaptivity that is the point" vs "leak-free but crippled" — **not "clean vs leaky."** The intraday-reset KAMA is a **deferred variant**, logged here so the reset-choice is on record as deliberate.

**Load-bearing no-lookahead / causality proof (hard precondition).** Trailing ≠ skipping the leak test. A **prefix-invariance test** proves `talib.KAMA` reads **past-only** (`kama(candles[:k])[i]` is byte-identical to `kama(candles)[i]` for `i < k` — no forward leak in the recursion), the same standard as the intraday indicators. **If it fails, the study does not run.**
