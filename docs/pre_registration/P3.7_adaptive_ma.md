## 0. Two variants owed (directional dichotomy) ‚Äî the coverage decision, explicit

Adaptive MAs are a genuine **directional dichotomy** (`MASTER_BLUEPRINT.md:327`, "cross or slope"). Cross and slope are **genuinely different bets on the same KAMA**, so **BOTH are owed** ‚Äî two specs, two runs, each pre-registered; **neither defaulted, neither deferred**:

- **V1 ‚Äî Cross (crossing signal):** position = which side of the KAMA price is on.
- **V2 ‚Äî Slope (trend-state signal):** position = the KAMA's own direction (rising/falling).

**They genuinely diverge** ‚Äî price can be *above a falling* KAMA (cross LONG / slope SHORT ‚Äî a bounce in a downtrend) or *below a rising* KAMA (cross SHORT / slope LONG ‚Äî a pullback in an uptrend). So they are distinct falsifiable hypotheses.

**On record (stated ahead of the result): if BOTH pass, that is a contradiction to investigate, not two wins.** The same adaptive MA cannot robustly signal continuation *both* by price-crossing *and* by its own slope ‚Äî they disagree in exactly the pullback/bounce regimes ‚Äî and have both net-profit; a double-pass gets investigated, not banked.

## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable) ‚Äî per variant

On the frozen large-cap **panel** at 5-minute bars, each variant produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** clearing the **two-part panel verdict** (aggregate seven-point gate on the equal-weight portfolio AND breadth: median per-symbol CPCV path-Sharpe > 1.0 and ‚â• 60% of symbols individually positive), net of the full Indian round-trip cost, realized-frequency annualized.

**Falsified if** the panel verdict is KILL/INSUFFICIENT, or the cost-viability pre-check finds the median gross per-trade move below the modeled round-trip cost.

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern) ‚Äî and honest low prior

A Kaufman Adaptive MA (KAMA) **speeds up in trends and slows in chop** (via the efficiency ratio), so it tracks a genuine trend closely and flattens in noise ‚Äî the "noise-adaptive" property the study is about.
- **V1 (cross):** price decisively on one side of the adaptive MA marks a directional regime ‚Üí trade that side.
- **V2 (slope):** the adaptive MA *pointing* up/down is itself the trend-state ‚Üí trade the direction it points.

**Honest low prior.** Trend/**continuation** family. **Every continuation bet so far has KILLed** (P3.1 V2 VWAP-cross, P3.2 breakout, P3.6 Donchian), and `RESEARCH_FINDINGS.md` ¬ß4.2 rates adaptive MAs "marginal improvement over fixed MAs; not a reliable standalone edge," so **KILL is a live and expected outcome for both.** One honest note, explicitly **not a thumb on the scale:** the **slope variant is the closest yet to the long-horizon-continuation direction** the P3.6 "continuation gradient" pointed at (a pure trend-state follower on a noise-adaptive MA = the cleanest trend-following bet in the slate). That is informative *context*, **not** a reason to expect a pass ‚Äî the gradient's read was "direction of least-bad-ness informative, magnitude firmly dead."

## 3. Specs (event ‚Üí entry ‚Üí exit/holding ‚Üí position)

Both in [`src/lab/research/strategies/adaptive_ma.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/adaptive_ma.py); both use the **trailing** KAMA (`indicators.kama` = `talib.KAMA`, the same period per variant).

- **V1 ‚Äî `AdaptiveMaCrossSpec`** (registered `adaptive_ma_cross`): per bar, `close > KAMA` ‚áí **LONG**, `close < KAMA` ‚áí **SHORT**, flat while KAMA is NaN (warmup) or `close == KAMA`. The position flips on each price/KAMA cross. Each day opens flat; 15:20 square-off.
- **V2 ‚Äî `AdaptiveMaSlopeSpec`** (registered `adaptive_ma_slope`): per bar, `KAMA[i] > KAMA[i‚àí1]` (rising) ‚áí **LONG**, `KAMA[i] < KAMA[i‚àí1]` (falling) ‚áí **SHORT**, flat while KAMA is NaN or exactly flat. The position follows the KAMA's own direction. Each day opens flat; 15:20 square-off.
- **Position size:** fixed unit (`strength = 1.0`).

**Design ruling ‚Äî TRAILING KAMA, not intraday-reset (operator, 2026-07-09).** Both variants use the standard trailing `talib.KAMA` (no daily reset). **The P3.3 intraday-reset analogy does NOT transfer:** (a) a trailing KAMA is **causal** ‚Äî reflecting yesterday's level at today's open is legitimate *past* information, not lookahead, so it is not a leak; (b) intraday-reset would **amputate the cross-day adaptivity that is the defining property of an adaptive MA** (KAMA's identity is adapting its speed to the multi-day regime) and discard the first ~50 min of every session to warmup ‚Äî testing a *crippled cousin*, not the indicator. So this fork is "standard, keeps the adaptivity that is the point" vs "leak-free but crippled" ‚Äî **not "clean vs leaky."** The intraday-reset KAMA is a **deferred variant**, logged here so the reset-choice is on record as deliberate.

**Load-bearing no-lookahead / causality proof (hard precondition).** Trailing ‚âÝ skipping the leak test. A **prefix-invariance test** proves `talib.KAMA` reads **past-only** (`kama(candles[:k])[i]` is byte-identical to `kama(candles)[i]` for `i < k` ‚Äî no forward leak in the recursion), the same standard as the intraday indicators. **If it fails, the study does not run.**

**MA flavor = a METHOD choice, KAMA primary (blind rationale), NOT a dichotomy.** KAMA (Kaufman's Adaptive MA) is *the* canonical efficiency-ratio adaptive MA ‚Äî TA-Lib-backed (no hand-rolled-indicator risk), the exact speeds-up-in-trends / slows-in-chop construct the study is named for; in standard usage **"AMA" ‚â° Kaufman's AMA ‚â° KAMA** (the same construct ‚Äî no dichotomy to manufacture). A *genuinely distinct* adaptive MA ‚Äî **FRAMA** (fractal-adaptive) or **MAMA** (MESA-adaptive) ‚Äî is a different indicator and a **deferred ledger variant**, run only if KAMA shows life.

**Frequency ‚Äî 5-minute as a CHOICE (with a mild lean, honestly).** A period-10 KAMA warms up in ~10‚Äì20 bars (~50 min at 5-min, ~2.5h at 15-min), so 5-min is **not mechanically forced**: chosen for cross-study comparability + the harder cost case + more session to trade, with a *mild* lean (a trailing KAMA gives more warmed-up bars per day at 5-min). **15-min is a deferred alternative primary**; when it runs it needs the **causal resampling step** (a 15-min bar unusable until its third 5-min sub-bar closes).

**Variant coverage (both axes).** *Direction:* BOTH owed (cross + slope). *MA flavor:* KAMA primary; **FRAMA/MAMA, a slope deadband, a fast/slow-KAMA cross (edges into P3.14), the intraday-reset KAMA, and 15-min** all deferred ledger variants.
