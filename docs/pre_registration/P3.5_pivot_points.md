## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable)

On the frozen large-cap **panel** at 5-minute bars, **fading price at the classic daily pivot support/resistance levels** — entering SHORT when price reaches the resistance `R1` (betting the level holds and price reverts toward the central pivot `P`), LONG when price reaches the support `S1`, exiting at `P` — produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** clearing the **two-part panel verdict**: (1) the equal-weight panel-portfolio clears the seven-point kill-gate (CPCV median path-Sharpe > 1.0, net of cost, realized-frequency annualized) AND (2) breadth holds (median per-symbol CPCV path-Sharpe > 1.0 and ≥ 60% of panel symbols individually positive).

**Falsified if** the panel verdict is KILL/INSUFFICIENT, or the cost-viability pre-check finds the median gross per-trade move below the modeled round-trip cost.

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern) — and honest low prior

Classic pivot levels (`P`, `R1`, `S1`) are computed from the **prior session's** high/low/close and are **widely watched** — many intraday traders place orders around them. The *only* plausible edge is **self-fulfilling support/resistance**: if enough participants treat `R1` as resistance, orders cluster there and price genuinely tends to reject it. So price reaching `R1` should partially **revert toward `P`**. This is a **fixed, pre-known level** mechanism — distinct from a computed intraday stretch (VWAP deviation P3.1, rolling-mean z-score P3.3) or a failed-breakout pattern (P3.4).

**Honest low prior, stated up front.** This is the **fourth fade-type bet**, and fades die worst so far (P3.1 V1 −18.8, P3.3 −27.0). Prior evidence (`RESEARCH_FINDINGS.md` §4.2) rates pivots "popular but thin empirical support; performs no better than random levels in most published tests," so **KILL is a live and expected outcome.** A KILL is also a quiet **myth-check**: it would be evidence that widely-watched pivot levels create *no* tradeable self-fulfilling effect on large-caps. The distinct (fixed pre-known levels) mechanism warrants one honest run.

## 3. Spec (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

Implemented as `PivotReversionSpec` in [`src/lab/research/strategies/pivot_reversion.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/pivot_reversion.py), registered `pivot_reversion` — deterministic and point-in-time.

- **Levels (CAUSAL, prior completed session only):** `P = pivot(ohlcv)`; `R1, S1 = classic_pivot_levels(ohlcv)` where `R1 = 2P − prevLow`, `S1 = 2P − prevHigh`, `P = (prevH+prevL+prevC)/3`. Computed from the **prior** trading day's HLC (via the proven `_prior_day_lookup` that `pivot`/`fibonacci_pivot_levels`/`camarilla_pivot_levels` use), **never the current day's** — constant intraday, known at the open, **first day NaN**.
- **Entry (fade at the level):** from flat, `high[i] ≥ R1·(1 − entry_band)` ⇒ **SHORT** (reached resistance → fade); `low[i] ≤ S1·(1 + entry_band)` ⇒ **LONG** (reached support → fade).
- **Exit (target the pivot):** a SHORT exits when `low[i] ≤ P·(1 + exit_band)` (reverted down to near `P`); a LONG when `high[i] ≥ P·(1 − exit_band)`; else hold until the **15:20** square-off. Each day opens flat; no overnight carry; **no stop-loss** (a deferred variant).
- **Position size:** fixed unit (`strength = 1.0`).

**Load-bearing no-lookahead proof (hard precondition).** A **prefix-invariance test** pins that the levels and signal at bar `i` are byte-identical whether or not later bars exist, with an **explicit mid-day-D check**: a prefix ending *inside* day D yields the same day-D levels as the full series (they depend only on the complete day D−1, so no same-day or future leak). **If that test fails, the study does not run.**

## 4. Pre-committed parameters (FROZEN before the run, set BLIND)

| Parameter | Value | ± one-step (sensitivity) | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| `entry_band` | **0.001** (0.1%) | ±0.0005 | enter the fade when price comes within this of R1/S1 |
| `exit_band` | **0.001** (0.1%) | ±0.0005 | exit when price reverts to within this of the central pivot P |
| position size | **1.0** (fixed) | — | no sizing |

**Frozen and chosen BLIND** (no data peeking). **5 configs**: base + `entry_band±` + `exit_band±`. Levels used: **R1/S1** (R2/S2 deferred). Registry entry (`STRATEGIES["pivot_reversion"]`), pinned by `tests/unit/test_registry.py`.

**Smallest overfitting surface of any study so far (a feature, not a gap).** Classic pivot levels are **formulaic** — no tunable lookback/window; the *only* knobs are the two proximity bands. So this study has the fewest degrees of freedom in the slate, which the DSR rewards: a pivot verdict is **cleaner evidence either way** precisely because there is almost nothing to overfit. This is the first study where the near-parameter-free structure actively helps.

**Frequency — 5-minute as a deliberate CHOICE (not a mechanical necessity).** Unlike P3.2/P3.3/P3.4 (whose intraday-reset windows are non-viable at 15-min), pivot levels are **prior-day-derived and available from bar 1**, so there is **no window constraint** — the blueprint even lists 15-min as pivots' traditional frame. Two principles pull opposite ways: cross-study comparability + "harder cost environment first" (more marginal level-touches / turnover at 5-min) → **5-min**; the strategy's traditional frame → 15-min. **5-min is chosen as the primary**, deliberately, for comparability and the harder cost case. **15-min is a genuine deferred *alternative primary*** (pivots' traditional frame — a real, viable alternative here, NOT mechanically excluded), to be run as a follow-up variant if 5-min shows life. **Build flag for the 15-min variant (when it runs):** the archive is 5-min only, so 15-min requires a **causal resampling step — a 15-min bar must not be usable until its third 5-min sub-bar closes** (no peeking at an incomplete bar), lest it introduce a leak.

**Variant coverage (method-choice rule).** **Classic S/R reversion is the primary.** Deferred ledger-charged variants (run only if the primary shows life): the **Fibonacci** and **Camarilla** pivot systems; **pivot-breakout** (trading a break of R1/S1 — overlaps the already-dead P3.2 breakout, so low value); **R2/S2** levels; and the **15-min** frame.
