## 0. A single self-contained study (not a both-owed dichotomy)

Momentum-pullback is **one** continuation mechanism: in an established trend, buy the *resumption* after a shallow retracement. Its natural "opposite" — a pullback that keeps going and *reverses* — is the fade family already tested and KILLed (P3.3 mean-reversion −27.0, P3.4 reversal −9.9, P3.5 pivot-reversion −9.8). So there is **no owed directional dichotomy** here; this is the with-trend re-entry, tested once. The choices *inside* it — how the pullback is measured, and enter-on-dip vs enter-on-resumption — are **method variants**, not directions: pick the best-motivated, name the rest as ledger-charged variants to run only if the primary shows life (§7), per the dual-variant rule.

## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable)

On the frozen large-cap **panel** at 5-minute bars, entering the **resumption of momentum after a shallow in-trend pullback** produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** clearing the **two-part panel verdict** (aggregate seven-point gate on the equal-weight portfolio AND breadth: median per-symbol CPCV path-Sharpe > 1.0 and ≥ 60% of symbols individually positive), net of the full Indian round-trip cost, realized-frequency annualized. **Falsified if** the panel verdict is KILL/INSUFFICIENT, or the cost-viability pre-check finds the median gross per-trade move below the modeled round-trip cost.

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern) — PURELY a-priori

True **before any result**, no reference to prior outcomes: large institutional orders execute in **tranches over time**, not all at once, so a real trend advances in steps; *between* tranches, price retraces on profit-taking and weak-hand exits. **If** intraday returns carry positive autocorrelation at the trend horizon, then entering on the **resumption** after such a retracement — rather than chasing the already-extended breakout — buys **closer to support**, a lower-risk entry into the *same* continuation. This is a with-trend bet that the move **resumes** — structurally distinct from the fade families (P3.3–P3.5), which bet the move **reverses**.

*(Guardrail: the mechanism stands on its own a-priori. It does **not** invoke the cross-study continuation-gradient observation — that is a deferred, forward-only signal, see §4.)*

## 3. Spec (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

In [`src/lab/research/strategies/momentum_pullback.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/momentum_pullback.py). `MomentumPullbackSpec` (`momentum_pullback`), symmetric long/short, intraday positions:

- **Trend regime** — `close` vs a trailing `SMA(trend_period)`: `close > SMA` ⇒ uptrend (long side active), `close < SMA` ⇒ downtrend (short side active).
- **Pullback + resumption** — an `RSI(rsi_period)` dip and recovery. **LONG:** in an uptrend, once RSI has dipped below `rsi_pullback` (a shallow oversold-within-uptrend), ENTER when RSI **crosses back up** through `rsi_pullback` (momentum resuming). **SHORT** is the mirror through `100 − rsi_pullback`.
- **Exit** — the trend flips (`close` crosses the SMA against the position) **OR** RSI reaches the opposite extreme (a long banks at `≥ 100 − rsi_pullback`). Each day opens flat; 15:20 square-off; fixed unit size.
- **Same-day-cross guard** — the resumption cross requires the prior RSI bar to be the *same IST day*, so an overnight RSI jump across the level is **never** read as an intraday resumption (the gap-as-signal trap the `intraday_zscore` docstring warns about).

**Causal / no-lookahead (hard precondition).** `talib.SMA` / `talib.RSI` are trailing (confirmed prefix-invariant), so the regime, signal, and position at bar `i` use only bars `≤ i`; a signal at bar `i`'s close fills at `i+1`'s open. The spec has a **prefix-invariance no-lookahead test** (`tests/unit/test_momentum_pullback_spec.py::test_momentum_pullback_prefix_invariance`) — a **hard precondition: the study does not run if it fails.** Confirmed **PASSED** (re-verified green) before this run.

## 7. Method-variants (ledger-charged, NOT owed — run only if the primary shows life)

Per the dual-variant rule these are **method** choices (not a directional dichotomy). Named here so the primary is transparent and the alternatives are **pre-committed, not cherry-picked later**; each is charged to the trial ledger if run:

- **Pullback measure:** `pullback_depth` band (retrace % from the rolling high) or a `swing_low` touch, instead of RSI-oversold.
- **Entry timing:** enter **at the dip** (buy weakness — a mean-reversion-timed entry) instead of on the **resumption cross** (buy strength-after-weakness — the primary).
- **Trend filter / frequency:** `EMA` or `trend_regime(fast, slow)` instead of a single SMA; **15-min** bars instead of 5-min (a lower-turnover, cost-flipping sampling of the *same* idea — clusters, ~1 trial not 2).

None of these run now. The primary is the single most-motivated construction (SMA trend + canonical RSI resumption).

## Result (2026-07-10) — COST-DEAD per §6 (operator ruling (A)); battery skipped — exploration-grade

> **Exploration-grade** (frozen-49, large-cap). §0–§10 are the blind PLAN; this block is the OUTCOME. Program ledger **unchanged at 46 → effective 6.12** — a cost-dead §6 logs **no panel stream** (same handling as P3.7-V2 slope). Not in the `RESEARCH_FINDINGS.md §4.3` scorecard.

**Cost-viability (§6): COST-DEAD — a compound finding, not a bare label.**

**1 — The blind base config is near-degenerate on real data.** At the base (`trend_period=50`, `rsi_pullback=30`), RELIANCE produced **5 trades in 11 years** (2,816 sessions) — median `|gross/trade|` 0.118%, a statistically hollow count. **Cause:** a dip deep enough to send **RSI(14) below 30 almost always also breaks `close` below `SMA(50)`**, so "in an uptrend AND RSI recovering through 30" is a near-empty set — only **0.9%** (28 / 3,288) of RSI-30 up-crosses fall in an uptrend. **Deep-oversold RSI and the trend filter are structurally near-mutually-exclusive.**

**2 — Cost-dead wherever it trades (pre-run diagnostic — data-informed, NON-blind).** To check the degeneracy hid no live edge, a diagnostic swept the pullback level to depths that actually trade:

| `rsi_pullback` | trades (11 yr) | in-uptrend crosses | median `\|gross/trade\|` | vs cost 0.182% |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **30** (blind base) | 5 | 0.9% | 0.118% | dead (degenerate) |
| 35 | 76 | 2.2% | 0.129% | dead |
| 40 | 586 | 5.9% | 0.127% | dead |
| 45 | 3,731 | 22.1% | 0.130% | dead |

At **every** tradeable depth the median gross move is **~0.13% < 0.182%** — relaxing the param does **not** rescue it; **the degeneracy conceals no live edge.** The mechanism is cost-dead on large-caps.

**3 — Ledger handling.** The neighbor levels (35/40/45) are **diagnostic (data-informed, non-blind), NOT scored trials** — no panel stream, **ledger unchanged at 46**, same handling as P3.7-V2. Only the blind base config was the pre-registered test; it is cost-dead → **battery skipped** per the pre-registered §6 rule.

**4 — Owned miss (lesson on record).** The unit-test fixture needed `rsi_pullback=45` to produce *any* entries — an early warning that the canonical **30** would be degenerate on real data. That signal should have been carried into the base-param choice instead of defaulting to the textbook 30. **Lesson for future studies: if a test fixture needs a non-default param to fire, sanity-check the base config's trade count on real data before pre-registering.**

**5 — Distinct from P3.7-V2 (same outcome, different mechanism).** V2 (KAMA slope) died on **turnover** — ~30,518 trades, per-trade gross below cost because it flipped on every wiggle. P3.9 is **degenerate at the canonical depth** and dies on **small per-trade gross at every tradeable depth** — not a turnover death. Both cost-dead; different failure modes.

**Disposition.** Cost-dead per §6, battery skipped, exploration-grade / large-cap; not in the §4.3 scorecard. **No re-spec:** a genuinely different blind pullback measure (e.g. %-retracement to a rising MA, no RSI) would test the *same* with-trend move and hit the same construction-independent per-trade-gross ceiling (~0.13%), so it would re-confirm cost-death — not warranted for catalog-completeness (a cost-dead §6 *is* a complete finding; the battery is skipped by design). Considered and declined the separate-proposal option; the evidence says it dies too.
