## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable)

On the frozen large-cap **panel** at 5-minute bars, **fading a failed intraday breakout** — entering SHORT when a bar pokes **above** the prior intraday swing high (by at least `break_buffer`) but **closes back below** it, and symmetrically LONG on a failed downside break — produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** clearing the **two-part panel verdict**: (1) the equal-weight panel-portfolio clears the seven-point kill-gate (CPCV median path-Sharpe > 1.0, net of cost, realized-frequency annualized) AND (2) breadth holds (median per-symbol CPCV path-Sharpe > 1.0 and ≥ 60% of panel symbols individually positive).

**Falsified if** the panel verdict is KILL/INSUFFICIENT, or the cost-viability pre-check finds the median gross per-trade move below the modeled round-trip cost.

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern) — and honest low prior

A **failed breakout** traps traders: momentum/breakout players chase a break of a visible intraday level, then price rejects the level and closes back inside the prior range. The trapped longs (on a failed upside break) must exit into falling prices, and stop-runs cascade — so a **decisive poke that fails** is followed by a reversal *away* from the break. The **`break_buffer` is mechanistically load-bearing**, not a spare knob: a single-tick graze of the level traps nobody; the buffer requires a *genuine* trap-inducing penetration before a close-back-inside counts as a failure. This is a reversal **pattern** (a failed continuation), distinct from a deviation **threshold** fade (P3.1/P3.3).

**Honest low prior, stated up front.** This is the **third counter-trend flavor** after the two mean-reversion fades (P3.1 V1 VWAP, P3.3 rolling-mean) both KILLed decisively. Prior evidence (`RESEARCH_FINDINGS.md` §4.2) rates reversal signals "weak and hard to systematize; noisy and prone to overfitting," so **KILL is a live and expected outcome.** But swing-failure is a **genuinely distinct trigger** (a pattern, not a threshold) with its own falsifiable mechanism, worth one honest run.

**Relationship to P3.2 breakout (stated ahead of the result).** Swing-failure fades exactly the breaks breakout would ride. Breakout KILLed (volume-filtered breaks did not reliably continue) — **mild interest** in "do failed breaks reverse?", **not** a licence to expect a pass. It is **not a direct contradiction** if it passes: breakout asked "do breaks *continue*?" over all filtered breaks; swing-failure asks "do the *failed* breaks *reverse*?" — a different population. A PASS still triggers the verdict-grade path.

## 3. Spec (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

Implemented as `ReversalSpec` in [`src/lab/research/strategies/reversal.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/reversal.py), registered `reversal` — deterministic and point-in-time.

- **Swing level (CAUSAL, intraday-reset):** the prior swing high/low = `intraday_donchian(ohlcv, swing_lookback)` — the prior-N-bar intraday high/low over **current-day bars only, excluding the current bar**, NaN at each day's first bar. This is the **already-proven-causal** primitive breakout uses (`test_intraday_donchian.py`): it **sidesteps the confirmed-swing-pivot lookahead** entirely (a centred pivot needs *future* bars to confirm — the exact leak that manufactures a phantom edge; we do not use one).
- **Entry (failed break, same-bar rejection):** with `ph = prior_high[i]`, `pl = prior_low[i]` —
  - **SHORT** if `high[i] ≥ ph·(1 + break_buffer)` **and** `close[i] < ph` (poked above the swing high by a genuine margin, closed back below → failed upside break).
  - **LONG** if `low[i] ≤ pl·(1 − break_buffer)` **and** `close[i] > pl` (failed downside break).
  - Everything at bar `i` uses only bars `0..i-1` (the level) and bar `i`'s own H/L/C (known at its close) → **no lookahead**; a signal at `t` fills at `t+1` open.
- **Exit / holding:** ride the reversal (forward-filled) until an **opposite** swing-failure flips it or the **15:20** MIS square-off closes it. Each day opens flat; no overnight carry. **No stop-loss** (an ATR/target stop is a deferred ledger variant).
- **Position size:** fixed unit (`strength = 1.0`).

**Load-bearing no-lookahead proof (hard precondition).** A spec-level **prefix-invariance test** pins that the signal at bar `i` is byte-identical whether or not later bars exist (the same discipline as `intraday_donchian`'s day-reset test). **If that test fails, the study does not run** — it is the proof there is no leak.

## 4. Pre-committed parameters (FROZEN before the run, set BLIND)

| Parameter | Value | ± one-step (sensitivity) | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| `swing_lookback` (N) | **20** bars | ±5 | prior-N-bar intraday high/low = the swing level (≈100 min at 5-min) |
| `break_buffer` | **0.001** (0.1%) | ±0.0005 | the poke must exceed the swing by ≥ this to be a *genuine* trap-inducing break (below round-trip cost, so a real-poke filter, not a huge-move filter) |
| position size | **1.0** (fixed) | — | no sizing |

**Frozen and chosen BLIND** (no data peeking). **5 configs**: base + `swing_lookback±` + `break_buffer±`. The registry entry (`STRATEGIES["reversal"]`) holds these exact values (pinned by `tests/unit/test_registry.py`). Every variant is charged to the trial ledger, K aggregate-portfolio streams per study.

**Why 5-minute is natural for this reversal specifically (mechanical).** The swing level is a 20-bar **intraday-reset** window, so a full 20-bar window must form since the open before any failure can fire. At 5-min ≈ 100 min (formed ~10:55, ~4h25m of session left). At 15-min it spans ~5h of the ~6h session (~1h left) — **mechanically non-viable for this lookback**; any coarser look requires a DIFFERENT `N` (a re-parameterized strategy, reserved only if 5-min shows life), not a "same strategy, slower bar" variant. Finer than 5-min inflates marginal wicks + turnover. Same shape as P3.2 breakout / P3.3 (all use an intraday-reset window).

**Variant coverage (method-choice rule).** **Swing-failure is the primary** reversal trigger. The other blueprint-named triggers — **RSI reversal** and **candle-geometry** (engulfing/doji at extremes) — plus a **multi-bar failed-breakout** (break and failure on different bars) and an **ATR/target stop**, are **deferred ledger-charged variants**, run only if the swing-failure primary shows life. No owed directional dichotomy (reversal *is* the counter-trend direction; the continuation version is breakout, P3.2).
