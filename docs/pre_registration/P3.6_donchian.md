## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable)

On the frozen large-cap **panel** at 5-minute bars, entering in the **break direction of the prior N-bar GLOBAL Donchian channel** — LONG when the close exceeds the prior-N-bar high, SHORT when it breaks the prior-N-bar low, the channel computed over the prior N bars **without a day reset** (so it persists across the overnight gap — a multi-session level), **unfiltered**, holding the continuation until an opposite-channel breakout flips it or the 15:20 square-off — produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** clearing the **two-part panel verdict**: (1) the equal-weight panel-portfolio clears the seven-point kill-gate (CPCV median path-Sharpe > 1.0, net of cost, realized-frequency annualized) AND (2) breadth holds (median per-symbol CPCV path-Sharpe > 1.0 and ≥ 60% of panel symbols individually positive).

**Falsified if** the panel verdict is KILL/INSUFFICIENT, or the cost-viability pre-check finds the median gross per-trade move below the modeled round-trip cost.

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern) — and honest low prior

A break of an N-bar high/low signals the prior range has resolved; classic Donchian/Turtle trend-following bets the move **continues**. The **distinct feature here vs P3.2** is the **GLOBAL** channel: it does not reset at the day boundary, so the level being broken is a **multi-session** high/low (an early-session break references the *prior* session's extreme), not today's intraday range. The tested question is therefore **"does breaking a longer, multi-session high/low continue intraday?"** — a distinct level and a distinct hypothesis from P3.2's "does breaking *today's* range continue?"

**Honest low prior + relationship to P3.2, stated up front.** This is a **continuation bet, and all continuation bets so far have KILLed** (P3.1 V2 VWAP-cross, P3.2 volume-breakout), so the prior is **low**. The specific, distinct reason to run it: a **longer, more significant multi-session level might hold/continue better** than the intraday level P3.2 failed on. That makes it **distinct enough to test — NOT likely to pass** (no thumb on the scale). Prior evidence (`RESEARCH_FINDINGS.md` §4.2) rates Donchian "strong pedigree on daily/higher timeframes (Turtles); intraday evidence much weaker and cost-sensitive," so **KILL is a live and expected outcome.**

## 3. Spec (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

Implemented as `DonchianBreakoutSpec` in [`src/lab/research/strategies/donchian_breakout.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/donchian_breakout.py), registered `donchian_breakout` — deterministic and point-in-time.

- **Channel (CAUSAL, global, excludes current bar):** `upper, lower = prior_donchian(ohlcv, channel_lookback)` — the max-high / min-low over the prior `channel_lookback` bars **[i − N .. i − 1]**, EXCLUDING bar `i` (so `close > upper` is a genuine breakout), **GLOBAL** (no day reset, crosses the overnight gap — a multi-session level, distinct from `intraday_donchian`). NaN until N prior bars exist. Reads only bars `< i`.
- **Entry (unfiltered break):** `close[i] > prior_high[i]` ⇒ **LONG**; `close[i] < prior_low[i]` ⇒ **SHORT**. No volume filter (the P3.2 distinction).
- **Exit / holding:** ride the continuation (forward-filled) until an **opposite-channel breakout** flips it or the **15:20** MIS square-off closes it. Each day opens flat; **no overnight carry** (the channel is multi-session, but the *position* is intraday). **No stop-loss** (a deferred variant).
- **Position size:** fixed unit (`strength = 1.0`).

**Load-bearing no-lookahead proof (hard precondition).** A **prefix-invariance test** pins that the channel and signal at bar `i` are byte-identical whether or not later bars exist (`prior_donchian` reads only bars `< i`). **If it fails, the study does not run.**

**Frozen and chosen BLIND** (no data peeking). **3 configs**: base + `channel_lookback±`. **One real knob = three configs — not padded to five:** manufacturing extra configs would be fake variation; fewer real knobs = a smaller overfitting surface, which the DSR rewards. Registry entry (`STRATEGIES["donchian_breakout"]`), pinned by `tests/unit/test_registry.py`.

**N = 55 is the textbook blind pick, not a reach for "more multi-session."** The **global** property (no day reset) — not the raw size of N — is what makes the channel reference the prior session, so 55-global already delivers the distinct-from-P3.2 test. 55 is the value chosen without looking at data (Turtle-classic); a longer N (~150) would be a stronger *version of the same hypothesis*, which is exactly what a deferred variant is for — reaching for it now would be a mild thumb on the scale.

**Frequency — 5-minute as a deliberate CHOICE (not mechanical).** A global channel has **no intraday-window constraint** (it forms from prior bars, crossing days), so — like pivots (P3.5) — 5-min is not forced; the blueprint lists **15-min** as Donchian's traditional frame. 5-min is chosen for comparability + the harder cost case; **15-min is a genuine deferred *alternative primary***, run as a follow-up if 5-min shows life. **Build flag for the 15-min variant (when it runs):** the archive is 5-min only, so 15-min needs a **causal resampling step — a 15-min bar unusable until its third 5-min sub-bar closes** (no peeking at an incomplete bar).

**Variant coverage (method-choice rule).** **Global unfiltered channel breakout is the primary.** Deferred ledger-charged variants (run only if the primary shows life): the **intraday** Donchian (overlaps the dead P3.2); a **volume-filtered** version (= P3.2); the **Turtle dual-channel** (55-bar entry / 20-bar exit); a **larger multi-day N** (~150); and the **15-min** frame.
