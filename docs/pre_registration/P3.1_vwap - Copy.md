## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable)

On liquid NSE large-cap equities at 5-minute bars, **fading intraday deviations from VWAP** — shorting when price stretches beyond `+entry_threshold` above VWAP, buying when it stretches beyond `−entry_threshold` below, and exiting when price reverts to within `exit_threshold` of VWAP — produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** that clears the seven-point kill-gate (CPCV median path-Sharpe > 1.0, net of the full Indian round-trip cost).

**Falsified if** the kill-gate returns KILL, or the cost-viability pre-check (§6) finds the typical gross per-trade reversion smaller than the modeled round-trip cost. Prior evidence (`RESEARCH_FINDINGS.md` §4.2) rates the standalone VWAP directional edge "weak and highly cost-sensitive," so **KILL is a live and expected outcome — and an honest one.**

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern)

VWAP is the intraday **execution benchmark** institutions size and grade fills against. VWAP-target execution algorithms supply liquidity that pulls price back toward VWAP: they buy when price is below their running VWAP benchmark (to beat it) and sell when it is above. This benchmark-driven liquidity provision is a real microstructure force that should make **extreme intraday deviations from VWAP partially revert** — a mechanism, not merely an observed pattern. The open question is whether that reversion is large enough to survive the ~0.12–0.20% Indian intraday round-trip cost; the mechanism is the reason to look, the kill-gate decides whether it is real.

## 3. Spec (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

Implemented as `VwapMeanReversionSpec` in [`src/lab/research/strategies/vwap.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/vwap.py) — deterministic and point-in-time (the VWAP deviation at bar `i` uses only that day's bars `0..i`; a signal at bar `t`'s close fills at bar `t+1`'s open).

- **Signal:** VWAP deviation `d = close/VWAP − 1`, using the intraday-cumulative VWAP of the typical price with a daily reset (`indicators.vwap_deviation`).
- **Entry (fade):** from flat, `d > +entry_threshold` ⇒ **SHORT**; `d < −entry_threshold` ⇒ **LONG**.
- **Exit (hysteresis):** hold the fade until price reverts to within `exit_threshold` of VWAP (a SHORT exits when `d ≤ exit_threshold`; a LONG when `d ≥ −exit_threshold`). The **wide-entry / narrow-exit** band means the strategy captures the reversion *toward* VWAP, not merely the excursion beyond entry (which would be cost-dead).
- **Holding / square-off:** intraday only; each day opens flat; positions are squared off at the configured **15:20** MIS cutoff by the backtester (data is regular-session-filtered to 09:15–15:30, PR #13, so no Muhurat/out-of-session bar enters).
- **Position size:** fixed unit (`strength = 1.0`). No sizing model in this study.

## 6. Cost-viability pre-check (before the full battery)

Per Deep Dive 02: before burning the full CPCV/robustness battery, confirm the edge can clear costs at all.

- **Measure:** the base-config backtest's **median `|Trade.gross_return|`** (pre-cost per-trade move) over the study window on the primary symbol.
- **Compare to:** the modeled round-trip cost fraction, `cost_model.round_trip_cost_fraction(100_000)` ≈ **0.18%** (from `config/costs.yaml`).
- **Decision:** if median gross per-trade move **< round-trip cost** ⇒ the strategy is **cost-dead**; record that as the verdict (a valid, logged finding), charge the trial to the ledger, and **skip the full battery**. Otherwise proceed to the full kill-gate.
- **A-priori estimate (NOT a result):** the wide-entry / narrow-exit band targets a captured reversion of ≈ `entry − exit` = 0.3% per completed trade, vs ≈ 0.18% round-trip cost — a thin but positive a-priori margin, so the strategy is **not obviously cost-dead** and warrants the test. Trades squared off at 15:20 without full reversion, and whipsaw near the band, will erode the realized median; **the measured value governs, not this estimate.**
