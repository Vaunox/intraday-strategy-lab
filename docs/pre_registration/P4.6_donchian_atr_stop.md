## 0. Blueprint combination

Blueprint P4.6 (RESEARCH_FINDINGS §4.2): *Donchian channel breakout with a volatility-scaled (ATR-multiple) stop-loss for risk control.* A minimal composition of the global multi-session Donchian breakout (P3.6) with an **ATR-trailing-stop exit**, **fewest knobs**. The ATR stop is *risk control on the exit*, not a second entry condition.

## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable)

On the frozen large-cap **panel** at 5-min bars, a Donchian breakout managed by an ATR trailing stop produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** clearing the **two-part panel verdict**. **Falsified if** KILL/INSUFFICIENT.

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern) — PURELY a-priori

**Sound risk control on a clean breakout rule:** an ATR-scaled trailing stop cuts losers early and lets the multi-session breakout (P3.6, the least-bad continuation bet) run, scaling the stop to each symbol's own volatility. Purely a-priori; honest caution — the stop improves **risk/drawdowns, not the entry's weak intraday base**; expect better drawdowns, not necessarily a passing edge.

## 3. Spec (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

In [`src/lab/research/strategies/donchian_atr_stop.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/donchian_atr_stop.py). `DonchianAtrStopSpec`, symmetric, per-bar stateful: ENTER on a break of the prior-N-bar GLOBAL Donchian channel (`close > upper` ⇒ LONG, `close < lower` ⇒ SHORT); trail a stop at `atr_mult × ATR` from the best close (a long's stop only ratchets up); EXIT when the close crosses the stop. Square-off each day.

**Causal / no-lookahead (hard precondition).** `prior_donchian` (excludes current bar) + `talib.ATR` are trailing (prefix-invariant); the stateful stop is a causal fold over bars `≤ i`. **Prefix-invariance test** (`test_donchian_atr_stop_spec.py::test_donchian_atr_stop_prefix_invariance`) = hard precondition, **PASSED**.
