## 0. A single self-contained study

The canonical fast/slow MA crossover — one **trend/continuation** mechanism (symmetric long/short). SMA vs EMA is a **method choice** (SMA primary, EMA a non-owed ledger variant, §7), not a direction. **Distinct from P3.7** (adaptive KAMA cross): plain, non-adaptive SMAs.

## 1. Hypothesis (falsifiable)

On the frozen large-cap **panel** at 5-min bars, holding the trend side of a fast/slow SMA crossover produces a **positive net-of-cost edge** clearing the **two-part panel verdict**, net of cost. **Falsified if** the panel verdict is KILL/INSUFFICIENT, or the §6 pre-check finds the median gross per-trade move below cost.

## 2. Economic rationale (mechanism, not pattern) — PURELY a-priori

A fast moving average crossing a slow one marks a **shift in the prevailing trend**; holding the trend side (long while fast > slow) captures the **sustained move**. The canonical "great in a trend, whipsawed in chop" rule. Purely a-priori.

## 3. Spec (event → entry → exit/holding → position)

In [`src/lab/research/strategies/ma_crossover.py`](../../src/lab/research/strategies/ma_crossover.py). `MaCrossoverSpec` (`ma_crossover`), symmetric, intraday:

- **Position:** per bar, target the **sign of `SMA(fast_period) − SMA(slow_period)`** — LONG while fast > slow, SHORT while below, flat during warmup.
- **Event:** the position **flips on each crossover**; squared off at the MIS cutoff (the adapter resets flat each day).

**Causal / no-lookahead (hard precondition).** SMAs are trailing (`talib.SMA`, prefix-invariant); signal at bar `i` uses only bars `≤ i`, fills at `i+1`'s open. **Prefix-invariance no-lookahead test** (`tests/unit/test_ma_crossover_spec.py::test_ma_crossover_prefix_invariance`) is a **hard precondition** — study does not run if it fails. Confirmed **PASSED**.
